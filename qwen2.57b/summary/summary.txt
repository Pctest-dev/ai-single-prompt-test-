The Northfield Community Centre renovation project began in either March or April of last year, depending on which council report you read. The project was initially budgeted at £240,000 through a £69,518 grant from the council's health and adult services fund. Some meeting minutes refer to this as the "original" figure for funding. Work was completed in December 2017 with most of the work having been completed by 2018. The main contractor Halford & Sons signed off on the job, although a letter from the facilities manager dated January suggests that some outstanding issues remain. Attendance at the centre has increased by 40% according to the newsletter and the council's own data shows a 12% rise in average use of the facility. Funding came from a mixture of sources including a government grant (from £36,785), local business sponsorship, and council reserves.
