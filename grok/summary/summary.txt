Summary:The Northfield Community Centre renovation started in either March or April of last year (conflicting council reports). The initial budget was reported as £240,000, though some meeting minutes cite £180,000 as the original figure (inconsistent). Work was completed or nearly completed in December, with the main contractor (Halford & Sons) signing off, but a January letter from the facilities manager indicated several outstanding issues remained (unclear final status).Attendance has risen by 40% per the newsletter, versus a 12% increase according to the council’s own data (contradictory figures). Councillor Davies called the project “a great success,” while Councillor Webb described it as “a costly disappointment,” despite both sitting on the same oversight committee (opposing views).Funding came from a government grant, local business sponsorship, and council reserves, but the exact breakdown was not disclosed in public documents (missing details). Multiple inconsistencies and gaps exist around timelines, costs, completion status, performance metrics, and funding.
