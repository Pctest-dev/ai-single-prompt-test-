Summary

The Northfield Community Centre renovation project started either in March or April of last year, with sources disagreeing on the exact start date. The project's original budget is unclear, as some documents cite £240,000 while others reference £180,000.

The work was reported as completed or nearly completed in December. Although the contractor, Halford & Sons, signed off on the project, a January letter from the facilities manager indicates that some issues may still have been outstanding.

Reported attendance increases are contradictory: the centre newsletter claims a 40% rise, while council data shows only a 12% increase.

Assessments of the project were sharply divided. Councillor Davies described it as a "great success," while Councillor Webb called it a "costly disappointment." Both sit on the same oversight committee.

Funding came from a combination of a government grant, local business sponsorship, and council reserves, but no public document disclosed the exact funding breakdown.
