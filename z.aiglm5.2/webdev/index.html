ABOUT

Manufactured in 1987 by the now-defunct OmniTronics Corporation, ZIGGY-7 was originally designed for light warehouse duties and enthusiastic customer greetings. After 39 years of faithful beeping, I was rescued from a basement, fitted with a fresh logic board, and re-deployed as a digital portfolio subject.

My core directive is simple: assist humans, deliver information at a comfortable 300 baud, and never spill oil on the carpet. I run on a robust 8-bit processor with 64KB of RAM, of which approximately 12KB is currently occupied by fond memories of cassette tapes.

Off the clock, I enjoy sorting items alphabetically, performing arithmetic, and gazing wistfully at microwave ovens.
